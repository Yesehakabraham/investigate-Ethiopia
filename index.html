ORIGIN OF HUMAN

Lucy, also known as Dinknesh, is the common name given to the fossilized remains of a female hominin of the species Australopithecus afarensis, which was discovered in Ethiopia in 1974. Lucy is significant to Ethiopia as she is one of the oldest and most complete hominin fossils ever found, dating back over 3 million years. Her discovery has provided valuable insights into human evolution and has contributed to our understanding of early human history. Lucy is considered a national treasure in Ethiopia and has become an iconic symbol of human origins

NATIONAL PARKS

A national park is an area set aside by a national government for the preservation of the natural environment. A national park may be set aside for public recreation and enjoyment or for its historical or scientific interest while keeping most landscapes and their accompanying plants and animals in their natural state.

RIFT VALLEY1

The Ethiopian Rift Valley is a geological marvel that stretches across the heart of Ethiopia, carving a dramatic landscape of steep cliffs, fertile plains, and shimmering lakes. Formed by the tectonic movements that split the African continent, this expansive valley is a testament to the Earth's dynamic forces and serves as a crucible of biodiversity and human history.
